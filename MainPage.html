A Guide to Finland

January 1, 2024, SH0D4N

The Land of a Thousand Lakes

Each lake in Finland tells a story of tranquility and serenity, reflecting the essence of the Finnish way of life. From the expansive Saimaa, the largest lake in the country, to the secluded wilderness lakes hidden among dense forests, these bodies of water become an integral part of the Finnish identity. In the summer, the lakes come alive with activity. Locals and visitors alike embrace the long days, basking in the warmth of the midnight sun, as they indulge in a myriad of water-based adventures. Boating, fishing, and lakeside saunas become cherished pastimes, creating a unique cultural tapestry woven with the threads of nature.

Chasing the Aurora

Finland, a country renowned for its pristine landscapes, vibrant cities, and unique culture, becomes an otherworldly wonderland when winter descends. As the temperatures drop and the days grow shorter, the northern regions of Finland become a haven for those seeking one of the most mesmerizing natural phenomena - the Northern Lights, or Aurora Borealis. In this blog post, we embark on a virtual journey to explore the enchanting north of Finland while scouting for the elusive and magical Northern Lights.
Our journey begins in Rovaniemi, the capital of Lapland and the official hometown of Santa Claus. Nestled at the Arctic Circle, this city serves as a perfect starting point for aurora hunters. In Rovaniemi, visitors can experience the unique blend of urban comforts and Arctic wilderness. The Santa Claus Village, an iconic attraction, offers the chance to meet the man in red himself and revel in the holiday spirit. As night falls, we venture into the surrounding wilderness, away from city lights, to increase our chances of witnessing the Northern Lights. Local guides, seasoned in the art of aurora hunting, lead us to prime locations, sharing stories of Lapland's rich folklore and the science behind the dancing lights in the night sky.
Continuing our journey northward, we arrive in Inari, a village situated on the shores of the vast Lake Inari. This region not only boasts incredible opportunities for Northern Lights viewing but also serves as a gateway to the unique Sami culture. The Sami, the indigenous people of the region, have a deep connection with nature and reindeer herding, and their traditions add a rich layer to our exploration. In Inari, we visit the Siida Museum to learn about the Sami way of life, their folklore, and the spiritual significance they attribute to the Northern Lights. Armed with this cultural insight, our aurora-chasing adventure takes on a more profound meaning as we search for the lights in the company of ancient Sami legends.
For those seeking a more remote and untouched Arctic experience, Kilpisjärvi offers a haven of tranquility. Tucked away in the far northwest of Finland, near the borders of Sweden and Norway, Kilpisjärvi is a small village surrounded by breathtaking wilderness. The nearby Saana Fell provides an ideal vantage point for unobstructed views of the Northern Lights. Here, far away from the hustle and bustle of urban life, we find ourselves immersed in the silence of the Arctic night. The Arctic wilderness in Kilpisjärvi offers a pristine canvas for the Northern Lights to paint their ethereal strokes across the sky, creating an awe-inspiring display that transcends the boundaries of the natural world.
As our journey through the north of Finland comes to an end, we reflect on the mesmerizing beauty of the Northern Lights and the unforgettable experiences gained along the way. Exploring Rovaniemi, Inari, Levi, and Kilpisjärvi has not only allowed us to witness the magic of the aurora but has also provided a deeper understanding of the Arctic's unique cultural and natural tapestry. Chasing the Northern Lights in the north of Finland is more than a visual spectacle; it is a transformative journey through a winter wonderland where nature's most enchanting performance takes center stage. Whether under the city lights of Rovaniemi or in the remote wilderness of Kilpisjärvi, the dance of lights in the Arctic sky leaves an indelible mark on all those fortunate enough to witness its brilliance.
